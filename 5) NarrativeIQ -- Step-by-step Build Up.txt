Step 1: Challenge the Idea Before Committing

Before we spend weeks building NarrativeIQ, let's attack it ourselves.

A project should survive three questions:

Question 1

Can it only be built properly using Data Warehousing concepts?

Answer: Yes.

Without:

Historical storage
Star schemas
Fact tables
Trend aggregation
Materialized views

the project loses its core intelligence capability.

This is good.

Question 2

Can it scale naturally?

Answer: Yes.

Start with:

News articles
Social posts
Comments

Then scale into:

Millions of records
Historical archives
Topic evolution

This aligns perfectly with Big Data.

Question 3

Will judges understand it in 30 seconds?

This is important.

If judges need a 10-minute explanation, you've already lost.

NarrativeIQ can be explained as:

"Our platform tracks how public narratives evolve across digital platforms. It collects discussions, news, and social content, stores them in a data warehouse, and reconstructs how sentiment, topics, and public perception change over time."

That's easy to understand.

Step 2: Defining the Core Problem

This is where most projects become generic.

The problem should not be:

"Analyzing social media."

Too generic.

Instead:

Problem Statement

Modern digital platforms generate massive volumes of information every second. News articles, social media discussions, and public commentary collectively shape public perception and influence decision-making.

Organizations can observe current discussions, but they struggle to understand:

How narratives emerge
How public sentiment changes
Which entities influence conversations
How topics evolve over time
Which narratives are likely to grow

Existing analytics systems focus on isolated statistics rather than narrative evolution.

NarrativeIQ addresses this gap by transforming fragmented digital content into a historical intelligence platform capable of reconstructing narrative lifecycles and generating actionable insights.

Step 3: Defining the Real Innovation

This is where we separate ourselves from dashboard projects.

Most projects answer:

Level 1

What happened?

Good projects answer:

Level 2

Why did it happen?

NarrativeIQ aims for:

Level 3

How did it evolve?

That becomes our innovation statement.
